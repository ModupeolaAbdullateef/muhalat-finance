Simple interest — what this calculates

Fixed income instruments (treasury bills, bonds, fixed deposits) typically pay a flat rate on your principal each year. This calculator uses simple interest: Interest = Principal × Rate × Time. There is no reinvestment of interest — the principal stays fixed and interest is paid out at the agreed frequency.

Muhalat Fixed Income — Common Questions

What is a fixed income investment?
A fixed income investment pays a predetermined, regular interest rate on a principal amount for a set period. Examples include treasury bills, FGN bonds, fixed deposits, and commercial papers. Unlike equities, returns are predictable and not tied to market performance.
What is the difference between a treasury bill and a bond?
Treasury bills (T-bills) are short-term (91, 182, or 364 days) government debt instruments. FGN Bonds are longer-term (2–30 years) and pay semi-annual coupon interest. T-bills are typically discounted at issuance, while bonds pay periodic coupons and return face value at maturity.
Is interest from fixed income taxed in Nigeria?
Interest income from bank fixed deposits is subject to Withholding Tax (WHT) at 10%. However, returns from FGN bonds and treasury bills are exempt from personal income tax under the Company Income Tax Act and the Personal Income Tax Act. Always confirm with your institution or a tax adviser.
How does a fixed deposit compare to a money market fund?
A bank fixed deposit locks your money for a set tenor at an agreed rate. A money market fund pools investors' money into short-term instruments (T-bills, commercial papers) and typically offers better rates, higher liquidity, and daily accrual of interest. Money market funds are generally considered low risk but not guaranteed.
Can I access my money before the tenor ends?
It depends on the instrument. Most bank fixed deposits allow early liquidation with a penalty (often reduced interest). T-bills can be sold in the secondary market before maturity. FGN bonds are tradeable on the NGX. Money market funds typically allow same-day or next-day redemption with no penalty.
Is fixed income a good hedge against inflation in Nigeria?
Only if rates exceed inflation. When Nigeria's inflation is running above 25%, a fixed deposit at 15% gives you a negative real return. T-bills and money market funds at 20–22% get you closer to break-even. For a true inflation hedge, consider combining fixed income with equity or real assets.
